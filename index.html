    Honda Shine Summary

    Honda Shine is a mileage bike available in 2 variants and 5 colours. Shine price starts at Rs. 80,407 in India with top variant price starting from Rs. 84,407. The Honda Shine is powered by 123.94cc BS6 engine which develops a power of 10.59 bhp and a torque of 11 Nm. With both front and rear drum brakes, Honda Shine comes up with combined braking system of both wheels. This Shine bike weighs 113 kg and has a fuel tank capacity of 10.5 liters.

    The CB Shine is a 125cc commuter motorcycle developed by Honda for the mass market segment. The Shine caters to a segment of buyers who are fine with understated looks but expect respectable performance and efficiency. This, coupled with Hondaâ€™s refinement and reliability, makes the Shine a solid all-round package.

    The CB Shine gets graphics that run all over the fuel tank and a 3D Honda emblem on the fuel tank. Honda has also introduced a limited edition model that feature cosmetic updates for the motorcycle to make it more appealing. It includes multi-coloured grab rails, modern side cowl and dual-tone paint schemes. The motorcycle gets a diamond frame chassis and is powered by a 124cc single cylinder air-cooled engine. The engine churns out 10bhp at 7500 rpm and 11Nm at 5500 rpm and is mated to a five-speed gearbox. The CB Shine gets conventional suspension with preload adjustment for the rear, along with 18-inch wheels. You can get your CB Shine with either a front disc brake or a drum brake. A combined brake system (CBS) is an option for both and comes as standard equipment.

        The Honda Shine is a popular commuter motorcycle known for its reliability, efficiency, and comfortable riding experience. Here's a detailed review of the Honda Shine:
        **Design and Build:**
        The Honda Shine boasts a simple yet stylish design. It features a sleek and aerodynamic body with attractive graphics and a well-finished paint job. The build quality is commendable, with sturdy components that can withstand daily commuting and occasional rough usage.
        **Engine and Performance:**
        The Honda Shine is equipped with a refined 124cc, air-cooled, single-cylinder engine. This engine produces around 10.74 bhp of power and 11 Nm of torque. While it may not be the most powerful bike in its segment, it offers a smooth and predictable power delivery. The engine is known for its reliability and fuel efficiency, making it an excellent choice for city commuting and occasional highway trips.
        **Ride and Handling:**
        One of the standout features of the Honda Shine is its comfortable ride and excellent handling. The bike has a well-tuned suspension system that can absorb minor bumps and provide a smooth ride. The handling is precise and stable, making it easy to maneuver through traffic. The seat is comfortable for both the rider and pillion, which adds to the overall riding experience.
        **Brakes and Safety:**
        The Honda Shine comes equipped with a front disc brake and a rear drum brake. This combination provides adequate stopping power, ensuring good control over the bike. However, it's worth noting that some competitors offer options for front and rear disc brakes for improved braking performance.
        **Fuel Efficiency:**
        One of the standout characteristics of the Honda Shine is its excellent fuel efficiency. It can deliver mileage figures of around 55-60 kmpl in city conditions and even higher on highways, depending on riding habits. This makes it an economical choice for daily commuting.
        **Features:**
        While the Honda Shine is not loaded with advanced features, it comes with essential elements like a digital-analog instrument cluster, tubeless tires, and a DC headlamp. These features add to the convenience and safety of the bike.
        **Price and Value for Money:**
        The Honda Shine is competitively priced in its segment, making it an attractive choice for budget-conscious buyers. Considering its reliability, fuel efficiency, and comfortable ride, it offers good value for money.
        **Conclusion:**
        In summary, the Honda Shine is a reliable and efficient commuter motorcycle with a focus on practicality and comfort. It may not have the most powerful engine or the latest tech features, but it excels in providing a hassle-free and economical riding experience, which is precisely what many riders look for in a commuter bike. If you prioritize reliability and efficiency in your daily commute, the Honda Shine is worth considering.
